# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard: Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project presents an interactive HR Analytics dashboard built using Power BI to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends.

The objective is to understand **what factors are associated with employee attrition** and provide insights that help improve employee retention and decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Identify key drivers of employee attrition
- Analyze workforce trends across different dimensions
- Enable HR teams to make data-driven decisions
- Provide actionable insights to improve retention

---

## 📊 Dashboard Snapshot

The dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of employee data, including key KPIs and attrition trends across multiple factors.

### 🔹 Key KPIs
- Total Employees: **1470**
- Attrition Rate: **16.12%**
- Average Job Satisfaction: **2.73 / 4**
- Average Monthly Income: **6.50K**
- Average Tenure: **7.01 years**

These KPIs give a high-level understanding of workforce composition and overall attrition.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 🔹 Attrition by Years at Company
Attrition is **highest in the first 2 years** and decreases as tenure increases.

👉 Insight: Early-stage employees are more likely to leave, suggesting gaps in onboarding or early engagement.

---

### 🔹 Attrition by Salary Band
Employees in the **lowest salary band (0–3K)** show the highest attrition, while higher salary bands have lower attrition.

👉 Insight: Compensation is a major factor influencing employee retention.

---

### 🔹 Attrition by Job Satisfaction
Lower job satisfaction levels are associated with **higher attrition**, while higher satisfaction correlates with lower attrition.

👉 Insight: Employee engagement plays a critical role in retention.

---

### 🔹 Attrition by Commute Distance
Attrition increases as commute distance increases, with the highest attrition among employees living far from the workplace.

👉 Insight: Commute burden impacts employee retention.

---

### 🔹 Attrition by Department
- Research & Development contributes the highest share of attrition
- Followed by Sales and Human Resources

👉 Insight: Attrition is uneven across departments, requiring targeted retention strategies.

---

## 🔍 Interactive Features
- Slicers for **Gender** and **Marital Status**
- Enables dynamic filtering and deeper analysis of attrition patterns
- Designed for user-driven exploration

---

## 💡 Key Drivers of Attrition
From the analysis, the primary factors influencing attrition are:
- Early tenure (0–2 years)
- Lower salary levels
- Low job satisfaction
- Longer commute distances

---

## 🚀 Recommendations
- Strengthen onboarding and early engagement programs
- Review compensation for lower salary bands
- Improve employee satisfaction through feedback and growth opportunities
- Offer flexible or remote work options to reduce commute challenges
- Implement department-specific retention strategies

---

## 📌 Conclusion
This dashboard moves beyond basic reporting by identifying the **key drivers of attrition**, enabling organizations to take targeted actions to improve employee retention and workforce stability.
